1.4: Describe the risks that might impact digital projects.



There are a lot of risks that can affect an online project, and depending on the kind of hardware/software needed, more risks are greater than others. For example, if a company uses a server, the company will have to make sure they have a backup server somewhere off campus or secure back up of all the data to the cloud. This is because of disasters such as fires and floods could destroy the servers and lose all the data, this is more likely to affect a company with a server trather than someone with a say a laptop. When planning a project, there are risks that can happane that we are aware could be a problem, and risks that we are not aware of. Over optimism is a very simple risk that everyone should be aware of and should take into account. Being over optimistic can lead to the project missing deadlines because the team bit off more than they could chew. There are costs risks that can impact a digital project and can be caused in many different ways. One example would be errors in a design plan. This could cause the project to give unexpected outcomes, which means more time is going to have to be spent fixing the code, in which the employees are going to have to be paid for their time. Another risk would be the loss of the data. This can be caused by a number of things, including an unstable internet connection or malware attacks. Precuations like backing up to seperate computers/server or the cloud storage, as well as training the employees with how to deal with unexpected emails and usb sticks. Copyright issues are also a risk to a digital project, but can be easliy avoided by using non-copyrighted resources and asking for permission from the creators.

1.7: Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects.



When creating a digital project, there are legal guidelines from the GDPR that are strict and must be followed. Depending on what the digital project is, different guidleines apply to it. For example, if the project is something that takes information from customers and stores it, it is that team's/businesses' job to ensure that the information of the customers are secure and well protected. If the databases get hacked and the information of the customners are leaked, the business/team will be held accountable for not being secure enough. Copyright laws are a big constraint when working on a digital project. Copyright infringement is a big issue of the digital world, but can easlily be avoided. To ensure that a team is not infringing on the Copyright laws, they must use non-copyrighted resources or ask for permission from the creators if you really want to use it.
