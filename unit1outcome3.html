3.1 Review the benefits and drawbacks of IT tools and systems used in terms of productivity and efficiency.



There are countless benefits of IT tools and systems used for efficiency and productivity, for example, sending an email to someone is more efficient and productive than going to meet a person that could live hours away and talking face to face every time a specific bit of information is needed to be said. However on the other end of that there are draw backs like, if a conversation is happening and a lot of questions are needed to be asked, it could take much longer waiting for and sending emails back and forth than a online time conversation. The lack of expession that can be conveyed through messages can also lead to misunderstandings and lead to relationships between two people to become volatile, which reduces efficiency. Another IT tool and system are things like social networking while doing work. Benefits of this include things like asking for help from a community on things like Github or Stack overflow as these you can get a lot of opinions and possible solutions from a bunch of people that might have more experience and knowledge than you. With these types of sites you can easily upload your work and try get specific answers that you might never find looking online. A drawback of this is that it is easy to get distracted by othere's things and lose focus of where you are at which can greatly stump productivity. Websites like Trello have lots of benefits to efficiency when it comes to planning , however with the lack of communication ability on there, it is easy for two people to accidently start the same thing which wastes a lot of time.




3.2 Describe ways to improve productivity and efficiency.



The ways to imporve productivity and efficiency are dependant on what you are doing. When it comes to coding in something like HTML or Pyhton, learning the different shortcuts on the keyboard and using tab to complete different words and such is a good way to increase efficiency when coding. The same goes for just doing somethign as simple as learing how to type faster on a keyboard. Making sure that all your files are organized so time isn't wasted looking around for the specific files you wish to open is a good, yet simple, way to increase efficiency and productivity. Something like making the files sorted into alphabetical order or just grouping relevant files withing sub folders. For example, if creating a game with two parts, have all the files releated to the first part in one folder, and all the other part in another. Anoher small one is to take regular breaks, people who take regular breaks will have more drive to continue than someone that works for giant periods at a time. A lot about increasing productivity can be to do with your mentallity and attitude towards working, taking regualr breaks will refresh your mentallity and allow you to push further.
